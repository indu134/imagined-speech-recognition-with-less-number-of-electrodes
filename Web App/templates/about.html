About Our Project

Our initiative is dedicated to advancing Silent Speech Recognition (SSR), an innovative technology designed to overcome communication barriers faced by individuals with neurological disorders such as Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS), Locked-in Syndrome, and more. SSR holds immense potential to empower those who are unable to communicate verbally by utilizing Electroencephalogram (EEG) signals.

Driven by a deep-seated commitment to improving patient care and satisfaction, SSR offers a transformative solution for individuals affected by conditions like strokes, traumatic brain injuries, Parkinson's disease, and others that impact speech clarity and volume. By tapping into the intricate neural processes underlying speech production, our research in EEG signal processing aims to unlock the potential of machine learning models such as Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory networks (LSTMs) to decipher silent speech patterns.

While significant strides have been made, challenges such as signal variability persist, underscoring the need for ongoing innovation in SSR methods. Our mission is rooted in the belief that everyone deserves the ability to communicate effectively, regardless of physical limitations. Through our relentless pursuit of advancements in SSR technology, we are dedicated to creating a more inclusive and accessible future for individuals with communication disorders.
